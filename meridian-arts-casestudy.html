The Problem

Why visitors
were leaving
without booking

The museum's existing digital touchpoints were fragmented — a dated website with no mobile experience and a phone-only booking system that frustrated modern visitors. Attendance was declining not from lack of interest, but from lack of access.

01
No unified discovery path Exhibitions, events, and booking lived in separate silos with no cohesive navigation, forcing users to jump between pages with no clear journey.
02
Mobile experience non-existent Over 68% of web traffic came from mobile devices, yet the desktop site offered no responsive design or touch-optimized interactions.
03
Booking required a phone call Visitors had to call the museum during office hours to reserve a spot — a massive friction point for younger, digitally-native audiences.
04
Exhibition context was thin Show listings lacked depth — no artist context, curated descriptions, or status indicators to help visitors plan their visits meaningfully.

The Solution

A platform that
puts art first

We designed Meridian Arts as a seamless two-platform ecosystem — a refined web presence for discovery and context, and a native mobile app that makes booking as frictionless as possible.

Responsive Web Platform

A fully responsive website that works beautifully across all screen sizes. Rich exhibition pages, event listings, and integrated booking — all with the editorial quality the museum deserved.

Native Mobile App

An iOS-first app designed for quick discovery on the go. Intelligent exhibition filtering, persistent event calendar, and a one-tap booking flow make planning a visit feel effortless.

Unified Booking System

A date-picker driven reservation flow that handles visit dates, times, and guest counts — with clear confirmations and a pay-what-you-wish admission model that removes financial barriers to attendance.

Final Designs

Every screen,
considered

The design language draws from the museum's physical identity — warm paper tones, deep teal, and Cormorant Garamond's editorial elegance — creating a digital space that feels as cultivated as the galleries themselves.

Results

Impact that
matters

3× Booking Conversion Rate

Online reservations tripled within the first quarter post-launch, driven by the frictionless mobile booking flow.

68% Mobile Traffic Captured

The native iOS app immediately absorbed the majority of visits, matching the existing mobile traffic share that the old site was failing to serve.

4.8★ App Store Rating

Users praised the clean exhibition browsing experience and the simplicity of the visit booking flow in early reviews.

−40% Phone Inquiry Volume

Inbound calls to the museum's front desk dropped significantly as the digital booking system became the primary reservation channel.

Reflection

What we learned

The best museum experience doesn't start at the entrance — it starts the moment someone decides to go.
Context builds confidence

Exhibition status badges (Open, Opens Soon, Always Open) significantly reduced user hesitation. Knowing whether you can see something today before you plan your trip removes a key anxiety for first-time visitors.

Friction is cumulative

No single step of the old booking process was catastrophic — but the sum of small inconveniences (phone call + office hours + unclear admission pricing) created enough drag to abandon. Removing each one compounded the improvement.

Design tone shapes perception

Usability testing revealed that the refined typographic approach and warm color palette made participants feel the museum was more prestigious — even before they booked. Aesthetic investment in the digital product elevated the institution's perceived quality.
